## Terminology Guide

### ALWAYS USE ✅

**Primary Terms:**
- "Industrial cannabis"
- "Cannabis sativa L."
- "Low-THC cannabis" 
- "Cannabis (THC <0.3%)"
- "Non-psychoactive cannabis"

**Application Terms:**
- "Cannabis fiber" (not hemp fiber)
- "Cannabis oil" (not hemp oil)
- "Cannabis hurds" (not hemp hurds)
- "Cannabis bioplastic"
- "Industrial cannabis materials"

**Product Terms:**
- "Cannabis-based alternatives"
- "Cannabis-derived products"
- "Industrial cannabis applications"

### USE WHEN CLARIFYING ✅

**Secondary Terms:**
- "Hemp (industrial cannabis)"
- "Commonly called 'hemp'"
- "Hemp - the low-THC variety of cannabis"

**Use these to EDUCATE, not as primary terminology**

### AVOID ❌

**Don't use as primary terms:**
- "Hemp" (without context)
- "Hemp products" (say "cannabis products")
- "Hemp-based" (say "cannabis-based")

**Never use:**
- "Marijuana" (wrong plant variety)
- "Weed" (slang, unprofessional)
- "Cannabis products" (without specifying industrial/low-THC - could confuse with recreational)

## Key Messaging Pillars

### 1. Scientific & Credible

**Message:**  
We use the scientifically accurate term "cannabis" because hemp IS cannabis - Cannabis sativa L. with <0.3% THC. This is not about getting high; it's about sustainable materials.

**Supporting Points:**
- Cannabis is the botanical genus
- Hemp is colloquial/legal terminology
- Industrial cannabis = non-psychoactive variety
- Used for materials, not medicine or recreation

**Tone:** Educational, factual, authoritative

### 2. Environmental Urgency

**Message:**  
Petroleum products are poisoning our planet with microplastics and CO2. Industrial cannabis offers biodegradable, carbon-negative alternatives that perform as well or better.

**Supporting Points:**
- Microplastics in human blood
- 450-year petroleum product lifespan
- Cannabis biodegrades in 3-12 months
- Carbon negative (absorbs 1.63 tons CO2/ton)

**Tone:** Urgent but hopeful, solution-focused

### 3. Legal & Accessible

**Message:**  
Industrial cannabis (<0.3% THC) is legal in 50+ countries including USA, EU, and Canada. This is about industrial applications, not psychoactive use.

**Supporting Points:**
- 2018 Farm Bill (USA)
- Legal throughout EU
- Non-psychoactive
- Grown for fiber, oil, seeds, hurds

**Tone:** Reassuring, clear, matter-of-fact

### 4. Superior Performance

**Message:**  
Industrial cannabis isn't just sustainable - it's often superior. 3x stronger than cotton, better insulation than fiberglass, more durable than many synthetics.

**Supporting Points:**
- Specific performance data
- Real product examples

- Industry adoption

**Tone:** Confident, data-driven, practical

### 5. Economic Opportunity

**Message:**  
The industrial cannabis market is $5.7B and growing 25% annually. This isn't niche - it's the future of materials.

**Supporting Points:**
- Market size and growth
- 25,000+ products available

**Tone:** Bullish, opportunity-focused, visionary

## FAQ Responses

### "Why do you say cannabis instead of hemp?"
"Because hemp IS cannabis - Cannabis sativa L. with <0.3% THC. We use scientifically accurate terminology. This helps destigmatize the plant and educate people that industrial cannabis (non-psychoactive) is different from recreational cannabis."

### "Is this about marijuana?"
"No. We focus exclusively on industrial cannabis with <0.3% THC - the non-psychoactive variety used for materials, not medicine or recreation. You cannot get high from industrial cannabis."

### "Is this legal?"
"Yes. Industrial cannabis (<0.3% THC) is legal in 50+ countries including the USA (2018 Farm Bill), EU, and Canada. It's grown for fiber, oil, and other industrial applications."

### "Can cannabis really replace petroleum?"
"Technically, yes. Cannabis can replace 80%+ of textiles, 100% of single-use plastics, 50%+ of building materials, and many other petroleum products. The question isn't 'can it?' but 'when will it?' We're accelerating the 'when.'"

### "Is cannabis better than petroleum products?"
"Often, yes. Cannabis fiber is 3x stronger than cotton, cannabis insulation has better R-value than fiberglass, and cannabis bioplastics are biodegradable unlike petroleum plastics. Plus, cannabis is carbon-negative and renewable."

## Legal/Compliance

### Required Disclaimers

**Always include when discussing cannabis:**
"Industrial cannabis (Cannabis sativa L., THC <0.3%)"

**On first mention in long-form content:**
"Industrial cannabis, commonly called hemp, is the non-psychoactive variety of Cannabis sativa L. with <0.3% THC, legal in 50+ countries for industrial applications."

### What We Don't Say
- ❌ Cannabis health benefits (not medical device)
- ❌ Cannabis cures/treats anything (no medical claims)
- ❌ Cannabis gets you high (incorrect for <0.3% THC)
- ❌ Recreational use information (out of scope)

### What We Do Say
- ✅ Environmental benefits (factual)
- ✅ Material performance (data-backed)
- ✅ Legal status (verifiable)
- ✅ Market opportunity (cited)
